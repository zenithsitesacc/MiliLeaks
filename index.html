Operation Outline

Operation Reference: EIFFEL-02
Date: June 01, 2173
Division: France Special Operative Branch
Document ID: 875-FAS-000-EIFFEL-RX1
Employer: Nestlé Corporation

Overview:

This document provides the overview and strategic mission outline for Operation EIFFEL-02, a special private-sector security operation to be undertaken by Peregrine International. 02 is part of OPERATION EIFFEL, a larger scale operation being undertaken within France. The scope of this mission is to be contained within the France Special Operative Branch and the mission to be conducted within the Administrative Regions of Paris and New Paris.

Specifications:

This mission is classified as a Type-5 targeted security operation.

Personnel are to be advised that under European Corporate Regulations, collateral damage will not be tolerated beyond the scope of ten civilian casualties or $30 million in non-target damages. Peregrine International is committed to limiting harm to private citizens in pursuit of serving its employers.

The Nestlé Corporation has specified that this operation is to remain detached from their brand name. As such, Operation EIFFEL-02 will utilize compartmentalized data and information structures. Only commanding officers will be informed as to the nature of the employer for this operation. Copies of this document distributed to lower-ranked personnel are to contain redactions of all mentions of the Nestlé Corporation, alongside any information that may trace to them.

Objective:

The objective of Operation EIFFEL-02 is the destruction of three Panard owned factories located on the outer sectors of the Paris and New Paris Joint Administrative Region. The employer has specified that these three food-manufacturing facilities must be destroyed as to be unsalvageable.

The employer has requested that casualties be limited. As such, strikes are to be conducted at night, before the workday begins, to ensure the lowest death-toll. The specific methods through which these three manufacturing factories will be destroyed is to be left to the commanding officers in charge of the operation. Higher command has however recommended the usage of high power explosives alongside chemical agents to contaminate the remaining rubble.

It is to be noted that these factories are guarded at all times by autonomous security drones, alongside private security staff. The employer has stated that the deaths of private security staff are of no concern.

Operatives:

Operation EIFFEL-02 is to be undertaken by the following Peregrine Assets:

Alpha Team: 24 Operatives

Bravo Team: 21 Operatives

Charlie Team: 19 Operatives (Security Assistance)

Aerial Support: 2 Fighter Craft, 3 Transport, 1 Overwatch, 8 Support Drones

Command: 4 Personnel, 1 Tactical Support Platform

Medical Staff: 3 Personnel

Intelligence:

Intelligence and surveillance states that each factory possesses a security team of approximately 8 personnel, alongside four groupings of Stokkman standard security drones. The personnel are believed to be contractors from Meridian.

On the first Monday of each month, at midnight, the drones are temporarily shut down for maintenance. The strikes should be conducted during this window.

Rules of Engagement:

Peregrine assets are to employ necessary force, under regulations 4 and 5 of the engagement handbook. If Peregrine assets encounter resistance, lethal force is sanctioned. Peregrine assets should first administer visible warnings to on-site security before commencing executions. These warnings must comply with the United Nations regulations on military engagement.

Mission Log - EIFFEL-02

Operation Reference: EIFFEL-02

2321 HRS - Commence Operations

All teams enter positions and begin Operation EIFFEL. Transports AZ-1 AZ-2 and AZ-3 touchdown. Command staff establish network. Aerial position secured.

2345 HRS - Triple Perimeter Established

Aerial control established. All sites contained within perimeter. Thermal readings confirm 9 active on Site 1, 7 active on Site 2, 7 active on Site 3. Assault teams move into position. Local surveillance shutdown. Transmissions cage established around all sites.

2357 HRS - Commence Operation

Drones enter sites. Drone 6 suffers rotor failure from undetected air-vent trap. On site security for Site 1 alerted. Sites 2 and 3 remain silent. Aerial support notes Site 1 transmissions cage suffering attacks from local security. Suspected hacking-specialized agent on Site 1.

0013 HRS - Insertion of Operatives

Operatives are inserted into sites. Site 1 transmissions cage loses frontal form and firewall. Command staff focuses on maintaining cage.

0016 HRS - Hostile Contact: Site 1

Alpha Team suffers ambush. Minor damage to Alpha-2, low calibre gunshot to the arm. Alpha Team commences firefight with local security.

0021 HRS - Hostile Contact: Sites 2 and 3

Bravo and Charlie teams perform successful attacks on security staff. Security staff ambushed and overwhelmed.

0022 HRS - Sites 2 and 3 Clear

Sites 2 and 3 local security neutralized. Bravo and Charlie teams commence deployment of explosives and chemical agents. Site 1 Security Staff transmits short range SOS through a crack in transmissions cage.

0028 HRS - Site 1 Clear

Site 1 security neutralized. Alpha team commences deployment of explosives and chemical agents.

0037 HRS - Objective Achieved

All sites cleared and detonated. Chemical agents and contaminants active on sites. Biohazard achieved. Commencing extraction.

0038 HRS - Hostile Contact: Site 1

Hostile contact with unknown Wielder on site 1.

0043 HRS - Alpha Team KIA

Bravo and Charlie teams extracted.

0048HRS - Bravo and Charlie Extraction Confirmed

Alpha Team KIA after engagement with unknown Wielder. Aerial support logged high surges of Abstract Energy. Ground transmissions seem to show Wielder utilizing a form of plant manipulation.

0135HRS - Casualty Report

Total Engagements: 4 direct combat incidents
Peregrine Casualties: 24 KIA, 1 major injury, 4 minor injury (non-combat effective status: 0)
Hostile Casualties: 23 KIA
Mission Status: OBJECTIVES ACHIEVED
